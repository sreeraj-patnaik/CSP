About the Project

A student-led initiative to understand internet usage and promote cyber safety across four rural villages of Vizianagaram, Andhra Pradesh.

Why We Started This

Rural communities in India are experiencing a rapid rise in smartphone usage - but cyber safety literacy has not kept pace. Residents of villages like Maharajupeta, Rajapulova, Jonnada, and Akulapeta are increasingly targeted by OTP frauds, fake prize calls, phishing messages, and account-hacking attempts.

Many victims do not know where to report or how to recover. This project was started to measure the scale of this problem, raise awareness at the ground level, and document what works and what doesn't - so future batches can build on this foundation.

How We Worked
01
We went to the villages
Not just sent a Google Form - we walked street by street, knocked on doors, sat with families, explained the survey in Telugu, and listened to their stories.
02
We asked the right questions
The survey covers internet usage, types of devices, connection quality, awareness about OTP fraud, and whether they had personally faced any online scam.
03
We held awareness sessions
At each village, after the survey, we sat with residents and explained common cyber frauds, how to identify fake calls, and what to do if they get targeted.
04
We put it all together
The responses are analysed and visualised so that the patterns are visible - who is most vulnerable, which village has the highest internet usage, what types of fraud are most common.
